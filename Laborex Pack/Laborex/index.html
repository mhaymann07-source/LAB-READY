Common Lab Chemicals
with Precaution & Storage

Hydrochloric Acid (HCl)

Corrosive | Use fume hood

Storage: Acid cabinet, separate from bases. Precaution: Wear gloves & goggles. Usage: pH adjustment, cleaning metals.

View Details

Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH)

Caustic | Hygroscopic

Storage: Tightly closed, dry place. Precaution: Causes severe burns. Use eye protection. Usage: Neutralization, saponification.

View Details

Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄)

Dehydrating | Strong acid

Storage: Acid cabinet, secondary containment. Precaution: Always add acid to water. Use fume hood. Usage: Catalyst, pH adjustment.

View Details

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH)

Flammable | Solvent

Storage: Flammable cabinet, away from ignition sources. Precaution: No flames. Use in well-ventilated area. Usage: Solvent, disinfection.

Acid Spill on Floor

  • Immediate Action

  • Evacuate if large

Laboratory Apparatus & Their Details
Beaker

Storage: Stack upside down on drying rack.
Usage: Mixing, heating liquids. Not for precise volume.
Type: Borosilicate glass, plastic. Sizes: 50-1000mL.

Erlenmeyer Flask

Storage: On shelves, avoid rim chipping.
Usage: Swirling liquids, titration.
Type: Conical shape, narrow neck.

Burette

Storage: Hang vertically or lay flat in drawer.
Usage: Precise volume dispensing for titration.
Type: Glass, PTFE stopcock.

Pipette

Storage: Vertical in pipette stand.
Usage: Transfer exact liquid volumes.
Type: Volumetric, serological, micropipette.

Bunsen Burner

Storage: Securely on base, gas hose attached.
Usage: Heating, sterilizing, combustion.
Type: Adjustable air hole.

Test Tube & Rack

Storage: Rack on shelf, cleaned and dried.
Usage: Small reactions, heating.
Type: Borosilicate, rimmed.
